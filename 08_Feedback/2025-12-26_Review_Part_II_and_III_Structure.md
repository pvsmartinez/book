## 3. Part III: The Filter (Goals & Structure)
*   **The Bridge:** Remove the "massive piece of the puzzle missing" section from Chapter 9. Move it to the *start* of Part III.
*   **Core Concept:** The engine explains *change*, but it doesn't explain *direction*.
*   **Terminology:** Start with "Selection" (more approachable). Slowly transition to "Value Function" (the technical/mathematical term).
*   **Part Goals:**
    *   **Part I (Introduction):** Mindset/Call to Action. Connect with the reader, salesman perspective, world feels "heavier."
    *   **Part II (The Engine):** Framework/Pattern. Show it's everywhere (living/non-living, internal/external). Teach the reader to spot the pattern.
    *   **Part III (The Filter):** Selection/Behavior. Explain how the filter defines purpose and outcomes. It's not evil; it's just the "fittest" for that specific judge.

## 4. Chapter 10: The Invisible Judge (Theory)
*   **Goal:** Bring the theory of selection together.
*   **Key Points:**
    *   Selection is not about good/bad/evil; it's about "fittest."
    *   **Previous Examples Revisited:**
        *   **Giraffe:** The savanna as an indifferent filter.
        *   **Virus:** Why does it become more contagious instead of more lethal? (Contagion is the value function).
        *   **Salesman:** Why do they become smooth-talkers instead of truthful? (Sales/Influence is the value function).
    *   **Technical Term:** Introduce "Value Function" as the mathematical name for this filter.
    *   **Quick Examples:** IMDB ratings, GPA/Test scores, Metacritic.
*   **Conclusion:** Understanding what is being judged explains the end behavior of the system.

## 5. General Principles
*   **No Meta-Bridges:** End chapters with a "payoff" or realization, not a "stay tuned" announcement.
*   **Standalone Scenes:** Each chapter should be a standalone scene.
*   **The "Judge" is Indifferent:** Emphasize that the system doesn't care about intentions, only "fit."
