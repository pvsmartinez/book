### Brainstorming for Chapter 16
The goal is to explain how "Optimization over Time" leads to a fundamental shift in the system (Systemic Drift), often reducing diversity or creating fragility.

**Option A: The "Gaming Meta" (Recommended)**
*   **Concept:** "Optimization kills Diversity."
*   **Story:**
    *   **Day 1:** A new game launches. 100 different strategies. Chaos, fun, exploration.
    *   **Day 30:** Players find what works. The "Judge" (Winning) selects the best tactics.
    *   **Day 365:** The "Meta" is solved. Everyone plays the exact same optimized deck/character.
*   **The Shift:** The rules didn't change, but the *experience* shifted from a playground to a factory.
*   **Why it fits:** Relatable to the author (Game Designer), neutral (players aren't evil, just wanting to win), and clearly shows how "Time + Selection" = "Extreme Uniformity."

**Option B: The "Desire Path"**
*   **Concept:** "The shortest path becomes the only path."
*   **Story:** An architect builds paved roads (The Plan). People walk across the grass to save 2 seconds (Optimization). One person does it -> no impact. 1,000 people -> a mud path forms. Eventually, the paved road is abandoned.
*   **The Shift:** The system (the path) physically changes based on the cumulative optimizations of individuals.

**Option C: The "Pug" (Biological Drift)**
*   **Concept:** "Selection for one trait breaks the whole."
*   **Story:** Start with a Wolf. Select for "cuteness/small size" over 100 generations. You get a Pug that can't breathe.
*   **The Shift:** You optimized for one variable (Size) and broke the system (Health).
