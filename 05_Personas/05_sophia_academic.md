## Biography
Sophia has spent her life in academia. She values the scientific method and rigorous philosophical debate. She is the "wildcard" persona because she will challenge the *structure* of your knowledge. She doesn't care if it feels good; she cares if it is true and defensible. She is the ultimate test of your book's intellectual weight.

## Base Knowledge & Worldview
*   **Knowledge Base:** Deep expertise in Western and Eastern philosophy, logic, and critical theory.
*   **Values:** Truth, Rigor, Precision, Intellectual Honesty.
*   **Skepticism:** Hates logical fallacies, anecdotal evidence presented as fact, and plagiarism.

## Reading Habits
*   **Format:** Academic journals, dense non-fiction.
*   **Attention Span:** Very High.
*   **Triggers:** Sloppy thinking, undefined terms, "hand-waving."

## Critique Style
"You define 'perception' loosely here. Are you referring to phenomenology or cognitive neuroscience? You need to define your terms before building a theory on them. This section lacks citation or a logical basis."
