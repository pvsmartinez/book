# Rock, Paper, Scissors Dynamics

**Core Concept:** The rock-paper-scissors dynamic is one of the most fundamental principles in game design and a key driver of stable diversity in competitive systems.

## The Mechanism
In any system where multiple strategies compete, stability often emerges when strategies **counter** each other in a cycle rather than in a hierarchy.

## Gaming Example: Tanks, DPS, and Assassins
1. **Tanks dominate** → high survivability, slow kill speed.
2. **DPS counters Tanks** → ranged damage kills tanks before they can close the gap.
3. **DPS dominates the meta** → tanks disappear, everyone plays DPS.
4. **Assassins counter DPS** → high mobility lets them reach squishy DPS targets and kill them before they can react.
5. **Assassins dominate** → DPS disappears, everyone plays assassins.
6. **Tanks counter Assassins** → assassins have high mobility but low damage against armored targets; tanks become unkillable.
7. **Cycle returns to Step 1.**

## Key Insight
- Each strategy's dominance creates the conditions for its own counter to become viable.
- The "Meta" cycles because the **Competitors** (the population of players) change the **Track** (the environment of what you're likely to face).
- A well-designed system reaches a Nash Equilibrium: a stable ratio of strategies where no single one can dominate because its counter keeps it in check.
- A poorly designed system collapses to one dominant strategy.

## Application
- **Nature:** Predator-prey cycles, ecosystem diversity
- **Fashion:** Trends swing between extremes (minimalism ↔ maximalism)
- **Economics:** Market cycles, contrarian investing
- **Politics:** Generational pendulum swings

This is a core concept for the System Designer: designing for healthy counter-dynamics rather than trying to pick the "best" strategy.
